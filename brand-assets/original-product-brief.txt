Know Your Bite — Meal Planning App (Product Brief, revised)
Intro — What this app is for
This app helps dietitians build fast, flexible, and truly personalized meal plans without wrestling with spreadsheets. You create a library of fixed-calorie recipes (each recipe has a precise kcal and macro breakdown). Then you assemble daily plans from recipe blocks to match the client’s goals. The app supports both precision clients (grams, macros, exact methods) and easy-mode clients (hand-measure portions). It also generates smart shopping lists, respects allergies/preferences, and keeps daily macro ratios on target.

Core idea (the heartbeat)
Fixed-calorie recipe blocks.
 Every recipe in the library has exact kcal + macros per serving. The planner mixes and matches fixed recipes, to reach the client’s daily targets.


Dual recipe format.
 Each recipe shows grams for accuracy and hand measures for simplicity (e.g., “1 palm chicken (120 g)”).


Automatic roll-ups.
 The app totals daily/weekly calories, macros, fiber, salt, and creates one combined shopping list for ingredients.


Real-life filters.
 Quickly find recipes by time, tools, prep style, dietary needs, budget, cuisine, storage, alergens etc.


Plain-language exports.
 Share plans and lists in HU/RO/EN, printable and phone-friendly.



Must-have features (plain language)
1) Tags & Filters (to find the right recipes fast)
Temperature / usage: needs reheating, oven, cold-friendly / no-reheat (office-friendly).


Meal-prep status: keeps 3–4 days, freezable, good next day, easy to box, portionable.


Time & difficulty: 10–15 min, 20–30 min, 30-40 min, 50+ min, one-pot, beginner, intermediate.


Tools: microwave, oven, pan, pot, pressure cooker, air fryer, blender, “knife & board only”.


Context: on-the-go, office lunch, pre/post-workout, breakfast, dinner, snack, lunch.


Nutrition goal: IR/PCOS-friendly, high-protein, low-fat, low-carb, high-fiber, low-FODMAP, gluten/dairy/egg-free.


Allergens: clear labels + automatic exclusion.


Cost & season: budget, mid, premium; seasonal (spring/summer/fall/winter).


Cuisine/style: Hungarian, Mediterranean, Asian, Latin, comfort, “fit/classic”, italian, chinese, american, japanese, Thai, Greek.


Ingredient logic: leftover-friendly, protein swappable, veg swappable.


2) Recipe metadata (so the system can “think”)
Portions & scaling (1 / 2 / 4 / 6 servings with auto ingredient scaling).


Per-serving nutrition: kcal, protein (g), fat (g), carbs (g), fiber (g), salt (g).


Dual measures: grams + hand measures (palm/handful/tbsp) together.


Shelf life & storage: fridge days, freezer months, thaw/reheat notes.


Batch-cook tips: how to cook 6 servings at once and box them.


Swap suggestions: if no chicken → turkey/tofu; rice → bulgur/millet (auto-recalc macros).


Tags from section 1.


3) Smart weekly planner (with macro guardrails)
Assemble days from fixed-kcal recipes (Breakfast, Snack, Lunch, Dinner are just categories; each chosen recipe has a fixed kcal).


Show live daily totals for kcal and macros while you add/swap recipes.


Macro guardrails per day and week with target ranges:
 Fat 25–35%, Protein 20–25%, Carbs 45–60%.


Add extra snack (choose any fixed-kcal snack recipe); totals update instantly.


Routine-aware filters: e.g., “no microwave”, “max 15 min”, “car-friendly” — planner only suggests matching recipes.


Smart repeats: mark recipes to appear 2×/week; planner spreads them and maintains variety (e.g., fish ≤ 3×/week).


4) Shopping list (pro and household views)
Automatic weekly sum across all chosen recipes; merges duplicates (e.g., onion totals round to “1 kg”).


Two views:


Pro/grams (exact totals, optional cost calc).


Household units (pcs/pack/can) for store use.


Grouped by store sections: produce, chilled, frozen, dry goods, spices, vegetables, fruits.


Local equivalents / RO–HU names: e.g., “brânză cottage / túró”.


5) Client profile & auto exclusions
Preferences: favorites, dislikes, religious/ethical exclusions (fish, beef, pork), available kitchen tools.


Allergies/intolerances: automatic filtering when planning.


Budget slider: nudges suggestions toward cheaper recipes.


6) Education mode (for no-scale clients)
Portion cards: visual palm/hand/tbsp guides by food group.


Swap chart: “≈20 g protein equals …” (chicken ↔ tofu ↔ cottage cheese) with gram values.


Hunger-brakes: add small fiber-rich snack recipes (fixed 50–100 kcal options).


7) Quality & analytics (safety nets)
Health limits: warn if salt > 5 g/day or fiber < 25 g/day, etc.


Variety meter: flag over-repetition of the same protein (unless intended).


Client feedback: “too fussy”, “ate half”, “loved it” → improve future suggestions.


8) Export & sharing
Multi-language exports (HU / RO / EN) for recipes, shopping lists, and weekly overviews.


Formats: printable PDF, shareable link, WhatsApp-friendly short list.


Views: Day view (with timings) and Week view (at-a-glance).


Two recipe outputs:


Precision (grams, macros, exact steps).


Easy-mode (hand measures highlighted, simple steps).



Day-to-day flow (simple)
Build the recipe library once (fixed kcal + macros, grams + hand measures, tags).


Plan a client’s day/week by choosing fixed-kcal recipes for Breakfast/Snack/Lunch/Dinner.


Watch macro % update live until you’re within targets.


Add or swap a recipe (e.g., extra snack) — totals update instantly.


Export the plan (precision or easy-mode) and the shopping list (pro or household).


Collect feedback; the app learns to suggest better next time.



Minimal recipe schema (content fields)
Name


Category (Breakfast / Snack / Lunch / Dinner)


Tags (section 1)


Ingredients: grams + (hand measure in brackets)


e.g., “Chicken breast 120 g (1 palm)”


Method (include batch-cook and reheating notes if relevant)


Per-serving nutrition: kcal, Protein g, Fat g, Carbs g, Fiber g, Salt g


Storage & shelf life (fridge/freezer + how to reheat)


Swap options (auto macro update)


Allergens (explicit)



Why this matters (benefits)
Faster planning: build from fixed-kcal recipe blocks, not from scratch.


Flexible for real life: easy swaps, repeats, add-a-snack, and strong filters.


Fits all client types: precision (grams/macros) and simplicity (hand measures) in one flow.


Professional output: clear bilingual recipes and tidy shopping lists.


Safer & smarter: macro guardrails, fiber/salt checks, and learning from feedback.
